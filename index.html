About

Nidhin Mahesh is a senior software engineer with 5+ years building blockchain protocols and distributed systems. He currently works independently on Fexr, a production-grade Web3 infrastructure platform that has grown from a single-chain custody solution into a multi-chain settlement layer with agentic trading engines and smart-contract systems.

Previously, as a founding engineering team member at Jupiter Meta, he contributed to the design and launch of the JMDT blockchain — an in-house zero-knowledge rollup on zkEVM — designing ZK circuit architectures and proof-verification pipelines. Before that, he co-led development of the Rubix Layer 1 protocol: core protocol services (P2P networking, Proof-of-Pledge consensus, asset custody), the network's first native wallet with Decentralized Identifier support, and the full migration of the protocol stack from Spring Boot to Golang.

Nidhin holds a Bachelor's in Technology from Amrita University, where he led the Google Developer Student Club and contributed through the FOSS club — ranked top-10 nationally for Google Summer of Code selections. He is the author of four peer-reviewed papers on blockchain security and decentralized systems, in collaboration with Amrita Wireless Networks and TIFAC-CORE in Cyber Security.

Core skills: Golang  ·  Rust  ·  Solidity  ·  L1 protocol design  ·  ZK rollups  ·  Self-custody wallets  ·  DeFi
